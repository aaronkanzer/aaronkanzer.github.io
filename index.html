About

Hello, my name is Aaron Kanzer! I am a Master's in Computer Science student at Northeastern University, as well as a Graduate Research Assistant within Northeastern's D'Amore-McKim School of Business Information Operation and Analysis Group. While not at school, I am currently the Green Monster Scoreboard Operator at Fenway Park.

Prior to graduate school, I was the 2016 Democratic Nominee for MA State Representative in the 2nd Barnstable District. My other past experiences include being a Policy Aide for the Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority, as well as an appointed member on the Barnstable Housing Committee.

Experience

Graduate Research and Teaching Assistant

Under the advisement of Professor Peter Tarasewich, I help tutor and create course materials for graduate and undergraduate students taking courses in information visualization and data analysis. I also assist the department with researching emerging trends in business analytics.

2016 Democratic Nominee for MA State Rep, 2nd Barnstable District

At the age of 21, I ran for the Massachusetts State Legislature within my hometown on Cape Cod. After knocking on over 8,000 doors personally, my campaign won a heavily contested primary and received 45% of the general election vote. My campaign was also endorsed by many elected officials and national advocacy groups.

Consultant / Web Designer

Since 2017, I have worked as a web designer with SignedOn on an ad hoc basis. Most of my work at SignedOn entails building static webpages with WordPress plug-ins, as well as cleaning and writing custom CSS Code.

Policy Aide

In 2014, I worked as a Policy Aide at the Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority. My work included conducting economic analyses on inventory and service-related issues, as well as writing SQL script queries to obtain empirical evidence for my projects.
